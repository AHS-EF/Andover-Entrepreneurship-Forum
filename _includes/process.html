Think

In the Design Thinking Process, this phase represents "Empathize" and "Define" (the Problem).

Empathize

To really understand the problem, you need to talk to people that are affected by the problem. In this case, it will be Andover High School students - that is your target market.

To empathize with your target market, participants need to engage, observe and interview a target customer (an ideal person in their target market). The information gathered is then organized and used to help define the problem.

Define (the Problem)

The goal of the Define mode is to craft a meaningful and actionable problem statement. This is otherwise known as a the POV (point-of-view) of your potential target market.

From the POV of individuals that are affected by the problem, your job is to come of with a clear and specific problem statement.

During your research, you might uncover some insights and problems, however, it is critical to "frame the right problem to create the right solution."

Plan

In the Design Thinking Process, this phase represents "Ideate", otherwise known as brainstorming.

Ideate - Brainstorm

Once the participant develops a clear and specific problem statement, it is time to brainstorm ideas.

A recommended transition is to create of list of "how might we...?" brainstorming topics that flow from your problem statement.

There are hundreds of brainstorming or ideation techniques. One we use at AHS is to sketch out as many ideas as possible within a certain timeframe, perhaps 5 minutes.

The goal is to generate as many ideas as possible, to come up with a "range of possibilities."

If you are working on teams, you can do this exercise on your own then share and merge your ideas until you come up with one grand solution.

And that is the end-goal, brainstorm from your problem statement until you come up with a unique and specific solution.
